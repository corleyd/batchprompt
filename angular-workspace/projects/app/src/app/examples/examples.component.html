Examples

BatchPrompt allows you to process Excel (.xlsx) files with AI prompts to generate insights, extract information, or transform your data. Upload your Excel file, create a prompt that references column names, and choose between text or structured output formats. Here are two common use cases:

Example 1: Structured Output - Presidential Terms

This example demonstrates structured output, which extracts specific data points into predefined fields. Ideal for data extraction, categorization, or when you need consistent formatting across all responses.

Sample Dataset (Excel File: presidents.xlsx)

A
name
George Washington
Thomas Jefferson
Abraham Lincoln

Sample Prompt

During what years was {{ '{{' }}name{{ '}}' }} president

Output Format

Structured Output with properties:
- term_start_year (number): The year that the presidential term started
- term_end_year (number): The year that the presidential term ended

Sample Output (saved to separate columns)

A B C
name term_start_year term_end_year
George Washington 1789 1797
Thomas Jefferson 1801 1809
Abraham Lincoln 1861 1865

Example 2: Text Output - Presidential Accomplishments

This example shows how to generate detailed text responses for each row in your dataset. Perfect for creating summaries, explanations, or detailed analysis based on your data.

Sample Dataset (Excel File: presidents.xlsx)

A B C
name term_start_year term_end_year
George Washington 1789 1797
Thomas Jefferson 1801 1809
Abraham Lincoln 1861 1865
Franklin D. Roosevelt 1933 1945

Sample Prompt

Given the name of a president, list the top 3 notable accomplishments (positive or negative) of the US president {{ '{{' }}name{{ '}}' }} during his term starting in {{ '{{' }}term_start_year{{ '}}' }} and ending in {{ '{{' }}term_end_year{{ '}}' }}.

Output Format

Text Only

Sample Output (saved to response_text column)

A B C D
name term_start_year term_end_year response_text
George Washington 1789 1797 1. Established precedents for the presidency including the peaceful transfer of power
2. Led the creation of a stable federal government under the Constitution
3. Maintained neutrality in foreign conflicts, avoiding entanglement in European wars
Thomas Jefferson 1801 1809 1. Completed the Louisiana Purchase, doubling the size of the United States
2. Commissioned the Lewis and Clark Expedition to explore the western territories
3. Reduced federal government size and eliminated internal taxes
Abraham Lincoln 1861 1865 1. Issued the Emancipation Proclamation in 1863, freeing enslaved people in rebellious states
2. Led the Union through the Civil War, preserving the United States as a unified nation
3. Delivered the Gettysburg Address, redefining the war as a struggle for democracy and equality
Franklin D. Roosevelt 1933 1945 1. Implemented the New Deal programs to combat the Great Depression
2. Led the United States through World War II, helping defeat fascism
3. Established Social Security, providing a safety net for elderly Americans
